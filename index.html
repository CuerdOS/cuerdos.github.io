About

CuerdOS is a GNU/Linux distribution focused on stability and constant performance on computers of any range, without losing any functionality.

This is not only a derivative with a customized desktop environment, but it has a series of optimizations and performance improvements, including: optimization of active services, improvements in the use of the processor, among many others.

Main characteristics

  • Debian based

    This provides great stability and security, as well as very good flexibility.

  • Light and fashionable

    Unlike other lightweight distributions, CuerdOS is a system that opts for the avant-garde.

  • Sway

    This distribution focuses on offering a great experience with Sway, although it also has i3 and Xfce.

  • Own repositories

    CuerdOS has its own repositories where packages that are not in Debian are provided.

Other technical features

  • Bauh Store preconfigured for easy installation of programs (Debs y flatpak).
  • ZRam enabled for better performance on low requirements machines but also to avoid writes to the SSD or lag times on the HDD by the SWAP.
  • Apt-fast pre-installed and configured.
  • Optimized CPU usage and RAM management.
  • TLP preconfigured for both Intel and AMD for better battery life.
  • Pipewire as a media server.
  • GRUB and boot logo customized to your beloved neofetch.
  • Polybar and Rofi (and Waybar) preconfigured and themed to achieve a good visual coherence together with the icons and windows themes...
  • Support for virtualization.
  • Automatic security updates.

CuerdOS Standard

This is the edition with the default CuerdOS environment, which has the Sway (Wayland) and I3 (Xorg) window manager.

CuerdOS Legacy

Originally, CuerdOS was planned as Xfce-only, an idea initially discarded but now revived as a dedicated edition.
